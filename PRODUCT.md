# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Men of all ages who want a quality grooming experience. Primary context: discovering a new barbershop, deciding whether to book. They care about their appearance and want a place that takes the craft seriously. Secondary: existing clients looking up services, prices, or to book again.

## Product Purpose

Bedbeards is a premium barbershop studio. This site is a brochure: it exists to build trust, communicate the brand's quality and character, and convert visitors into booked clients. Success means a visitor who lands on any page leaves with a clear sense of what Bedbeards is, who works there, and a reason to walk through the door.

## Brand Personality

Rugged, Premium, Precise. The tone is confident without being loud. The brand lives in the craft — every detail deliberate, nothing superfluous. Warmth comes through the people (team, testimonials), not through a bright or playful aesthetic.

## Anti-references

- Generic franchise barbershops (Sports Clips, Great Clips): flat, corporate, template-feeling. No stock-photo warmth, no franchise-bold orange CTAs.
- Bright, colorful, or playful interfaces: this is a serious grooming studio. Nothing cheerful, high-saturation, or casual.

## Design Principles

1. **The craft is the story.** Every section should feel as considered as a precision cut. No filler sections, no placeholder copy, no generic layout patterns.
2. **Dark confidence.** The dark palette is a feature, not a preference. It signals premium, focused, intentional. Never dilute it with light backgrounds or cheerful accents.
3. **Precision over decoration.** Ornament that doesn't carry meaning gets cut. Motion, spacing, and typography do the work that decoration usually does.
4. **People are the product.** The barbers, their work, their clients' reactions — these are the site's most powerful content. Surface them early and often.
5. **Broad appeal, uncompromising quality.** The site should feel approachable to any man who walks in, but it should never feel generic. Quality signals matter: photography, typography, copy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All text at ≥ 4.5:1 contrast against its background. Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Images carry meaningful alt text.
